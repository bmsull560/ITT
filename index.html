What is IIP?

The International Investment Position (IIP) measures the balance of U.S. assets abroad and foreign investments in the U.S., offering a comprehensive picture of the nation’s global financial relationships.

Historical Trends

1980s: Positive IIP, as the U.S. was a net creditor nation.

2000s: Growing deficits turned the U.S. into a net debtor nation.

2024: A negative IIP of –$22.52 trillion, reflecting the significant foreign ownership of U.S. assets.
